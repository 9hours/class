What are Keyboard Shortcuts?

As the term describes, it's a key or a combination of keys that provide quick access to a particular action on your computer. For example, CMD + Z/CTRL + Z undoes something.

Why change it in Photoshop?

Short answer: The default is counterintuitive! To customize for your own needs! To be more efficient! To be more comfortable with the program!

Long answer: CMD + Z / CTRL + Z acts both as an undo and a redo action. Meanwhile, in let's say Microsoft Word, undo can be performed multiple times. It is possible to perform multiple undo actions, or a Step Backwards as Photoshop calls it, however, an additional key must be pressed.

Let's Start!
How to change Keyboard Shortcuts

STEP 1

  1. Go to Edit.
  2. Go all the way down to
    Keyboard Shortcuts... and click.
  3. RESULT: a new window will open.

STEP 2

  1. Make sure the Keyboard Shortcuts tab is selected.
  2. Under the Application Menu Command, find and click on Edit.
  3. RESULT: the Edit drop down will open, you'll see keyboard shortcut names such as Undo/Redo.

STEP 3

  1. Under Edit, go to Step Backward. Next to it is its corresponding shortcut.
  2. Click on it to assign your desired shortcut command. In the example, I used CMD + Z / CTRL + Z.
  3. Click Accept, then OK, and now you're set!

what are history states?

A history state is a change during your working session. Older states are automatically deleted to free more memory for Photoshop.

By default, the history panel lists the previous 20 states for older versions Photoshop such as CS5. For newer ones, the default is 50 history states.

why change the history states?

Usually for convenience. When working on larger projects, it’s more likely that we’ll be doing multiple small changes or we might not be paying attention so having large history states enables us to go further back in the project.

If you want to learn more about the undo and history, you can go here: HERE

Let's Start!
How to increase history states

STEP 1

  1. IN APPLE: go to Photoshop in the upper left corner. Next, go to Preferences, then General and click.
  2. IN PC: Since there's no Photoshop in the menu bar, it's located under Edit. Go all the way down to Preferences, then General and click.
  3. RESULT: A new window will pop up.

STEP 2

  1. On the left hand side, find and click on Performance.
  2. Under the section History & Cache, go to History States and increase the number to any amount. I went with 100.
  3. Then click OK and you're done!
